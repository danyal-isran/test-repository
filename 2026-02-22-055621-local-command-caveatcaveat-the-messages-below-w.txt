  VIII. Failure Swing Detection (Your Key Edge)

  This is the pattern you keep referencing and it deserves its own section
  because it's both your best signal AND your biggest trap when misread.

  High Probability Failure Swing (tradeable)

  1. Price pushes to make a new high/low on 5m
  2. The candle closes INSIDE the prior range (doesn't hold the break)
  3. The next candle immediately reverses (displacement in the opposite
  direction)
  4. Volume confirms (the failure candle has above-average volume)

  How to use it:
  - If you're long and the 5m prints a failure swing at HOD → exit. Don't wait.
  - If SPY fails to make HOD on LTF → anticipate failure swing to downside →
  look for which name is STILL holding above 20 EMA → that's your next long
  entry candidate after the shakeout

  Low Probability Failure Swing (avoid)

  - Price fails to make a new high but is still ABOVE the 20 EMA on 5m
  - The "failure" happens in the middle of a range, not at an extreme
  - There's no displacement candle confirming the reversal
  - This is just normal ranging behavior, not a tradeable signal

  IX. Addressing Your Specific Weak Points

  Problem 1: Entering After Distribution

  You wrote: "You are entering once we've already distributed to daily target"

  Rule: Before entering, check — has price already moved 70%+ of its average
  daily range (ADR)? If yes, the easy money is gone. Either wait for the next
  session or look for a different name that hasn't moved yet.

  Calculate ADR for each name in your watchlist weekly. Know the number.

  Problem 2: Entering When 5m Has Already Distributed

  You wrote: "You seem to be entering on FVGs when 5m has already distributed"

  Rule: Only enter FVGs when the 5m is in the FIRST LEG of a move (first push
  out of a range or first pullback after a break of structure). If you're seeing
   the 3rd or 4th FVG in a sequence, the move is exhausted.

  Problem 3: Lack of Follow-Through Fear

  You wrote: "My biggest hesitation fear is lack of follow through"

  This is solved by:
  1. Only trading when alignment is confirmed across timeframes (Section IV
  checklist)
  2. Using Model C (Visions) when uncertain — the extra confirmation reduces the
   false signal rate
  3. Accepting that some trades will stop you out before working. Your stop is
  the COST of finding out if the move is real. If your edge is present and you
  execute consistently, the math works over 50+ trades.

  Problem 4: Impulsive Entries (AMZN 02/12)

  You described chasing after seeing price fall through your level.

  Rule: If you missed the planned entry by more than 30 seconds, the trade is
  GONE. Do not chase. Write "missed — clean entry" in your journal and wait for
  the next setup. There will always be another one.

  Problem 5: Wrong Name Selection

  You listed this as a failure mode but had a "?" — you're not sure when it's
