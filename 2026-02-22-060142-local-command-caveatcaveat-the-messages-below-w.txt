⏺ Your observation aligns with common market behavior. A few points:

  Why this tends to be true:

  1. Liquidity exhaustion - After sweeping 4h lows, the move toward highs
  consumes the available liquidity. Once price reaches the opposing liquidity
  pool (4h highs), there may not be enough fuel to continue.
  2. Target = decision point - Pre-market 4h highs are significant levels where
  larger players take profits or initiate counter-positions. A rejection there
  signals the move has fulfilled its purpose.
  3. Trend structure breaks down - Clean 5m trends rely on consistent higher
  lows (or lower highs). A rejection at target often creates the first lower
  high, breaking the pattern that made the 20 EMA "clean."
  4. Chop = indecision - After a target rejection, you get two-sided flow: longs
   taking profit, shorts entering. This creates the overlapping candles and EMA
  whipsaws you're describing.

  Practical takeaway:

  Your edge is likely strongest in the move to target, not through it. Once
  price reaches 4h highs pre-market and shows rejection (wick, bearish
  engulfing, failed breakout), the setup has played out. Continuing to trade the
   same direction in the chop that follows is fighting changed conditions.

  Some traders use target rejection as a signal to:
  - Close remaining position
  - Wait for new structure to form
  - Or even look for reversal setups if rejection is strong

  Does this match what you're seeing in your backtests/live observations?

  It's simple math:

  Distance to stop (risk) vs Distance to target (reward)

  Example using your setup:

  Price sweeps 4H low at 590.00
  Price reclaims to 590.50
  4H high (target) at 594.00

  Stop: Below sweep → 589.80 (0.70 risk)
  Target: 4H high → 594.00 (3.50 reward)

  R:R = 3.50 / 0.70 = 5:1

  5:1 R:R = "makes sense" → take starters even without 5m confirmation

  ├─────────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤

  □ First 5m candle low

  Step 6: Calculate R:R

  Target = Next daily level (PDH, PDL, range H/L, gap fill)
  Stop = Below/above entry candle or sweep low/high
  Entry = Current price

  R:R = (Target - Entry) / (Entry - Stop)

  ┌───────┬──────────────────────────────────────────┐
  │  R:R  │                  Action                  │
  ├───────┼──────────────────────────────────────────┤

  ├───────┼──────────────────────────────────────────┤
  │ < 2:1 │ Skip - not worth it                      │
  └───────┴──────────────────────────────────────────┘

  □ Any daily levels in the way?
  □ If level is close → skip or reduce size

  Requirements:
  - All of Path A, plus:
  - 5m full body closes outside opening range
  - EMA aligned with direction

  Action:

  Stop below entry candle low (not the level)

❯ what is a run zone? 

⏺ A run zone is empty space on the daily chart between two levels - an area with
   no support/resistance where price can move freely.

  ---
  Visual

  Daily Chart:

  595.00 ──────── Level (resistance)

         [NO LEVELS HERE]     ← Run Zone
         [Empty space]
         [Price runs freely]

  585.00 ──────── Level (support)

  ---
  How to Spot It

  Kai's tip:
  "Tip to identify a run is you zoom out and see a straight line"

  No candle bodies, no wicks, no price action in that zone = run zone.

  ---
  Why It Matters

  ┌───────────────────────┬────────────────────────┐
  │       Run Zone        │      No Run Zone       │
  ├───────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │ Nothing to stop price │ Levels cause rejection │
  ├───────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │ Cleaner move          │ Choppy move            │
  ├───────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │ Better R:R            │ Worse R:R              │
  ├───────────────────────┼────────────────────────┤
  │ Take the trade        │ Skip or be cautious    │
  └───────────────────────┴────────────────────────┘

  ---
  Example

  PDH: 594.00
  PDL: 590.00

  If no levels between them → 590 to 594 is a run zone
  If there's a level at 592 → run zone is smaller, could reject at 592
